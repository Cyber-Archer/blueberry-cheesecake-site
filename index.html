The Story Behind the Cheesecake

Did you know cheesecake dates back to ancient Greece? But for me, it started in my grandmother’s hands. She learned the recipe from her mother, who adapted it from an old European cookbook. Over time, she perfected it, making it her own with a special blueberry compote that added just the right amount of tartness to the sweet, creamy base.

I feel others would appreciate my grandmother’s blueberry cheesecake as much as I do because it’s more than just a dessert—it’s a story, a tradition, and a bite of nostalgia. The rich, creamy texture of the cheesecake paired with the tart burst of homemade blueberry compote creates a perfect balance of flavors. Every ingredient, every step, was chosen with care, making each slice feel like a warm hug. Whether you love desserts, appreciate homemade recipes, or just enjoy a moment of comfort, this cheesecake has a way of turning any ordinary day into something special.

Grandma’s Blueberry Cheesecake Recipe

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Cheesecake Filling:

  • 3 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour

For the Blueberry Topping:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen blueberries
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Instructions:

Prepare the Crust:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C). In a bowl, mix graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Press into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then set aside to cool.

Make the Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth. Add sugar and vanilla, then beat until combined.
  2. Add eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed. Stir in sour cream and flour until just blended.

Bake the Cheesecake:

  1. Pour the filling over the cooled crust. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the center is set but slightly jiggly.
  2. Turn off the oven and let the cheesecake sit inside with the door slightly open for an hour.

Prepare the Blueberry Topping:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine blueberries, sugar, cornstarch, water, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens and the berries burst (about 5-7 minutes). Let cool.

Chill & Serve:

  1. Once the cheesecake is at room temperature, refrigerate for at least 4 hours (preferably overnight).
  2. Top with the blueberry compote before serving.
